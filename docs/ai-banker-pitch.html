The Three Pillars
What makes a good banker?
A banker who's good at their job does three things. Today, no digital banking product does all three. Most don't do any of them well.
👁

Conscience

Everything the banker knows about you before you say a word. Derived from transaction data — what you spend, where, when, how often, how it's changing.

  • Hidden totals nobody sees pile up
  • Spending creep that grew too slowly to notice
  • Frequency blindness — 18 orders in 30 days
  • Time signatures — 28% of spend in a 2-hour window
  • The shape of your month tells a story
💬

Companionship

Everything the banker learns from talking to you. Turns a data engine into a relationship. Knows what you value, what you resent, what to protect.

  • Structured decisions — goal, pace, sacred categories
  • Extracted preferences — "don't touch my SIPs"
  • Spend sentiment — joy vs. regret vs. meh
  • Context that data alone can't capture
  • Memory that persists across every session
⚡

Commerce

The banker's ability to act. Not just observe and advise — execute. Set up an RD, start autosave, adjust a budget, lock surplus in an FD.

  • Today: user confirms every action via banking UI
  • Near-term: pre-authorized action types
  • Long-term: autonomous within boundaries
  • Trust is earned, not assumed
  • Understanding is valuable before execution matures

Commerce Strategy
Trust is earned, not assumed
The initial goal is to maximize the depth of Conscience and Companionship. Commerce starts with the user firmly in control and grows as trust builds.
1

Present: User confirms every action

The AI does all the analysis, builds the recommendation, and prepares the action. The final step is always the user's. When someone confirms a savings product, we drop them on the summary screen of the existing banking flow. They tap confirm. The button press is theirs.

Maximize Conscience + Companionship
2

Near-term: Pre-authorized actions

User opts into specific action types: "auto-save ₹70/day, don't ask me each time." The banker acts within explicit boundaries. User gets a receipt, not a permission prompt.

Earned autonomy
3

Long-term: Autonomous within boundaries

The banker proactively moves money, adjusts budgets, parks surplus — all within pre-authorized rules. User reviews after the fact. A real banker, not a chatbot.

Full commerce
The product is designed to be valuable even if Commerce never goes beyond "user taps confirm."
Understanding your money better and having a financial companion that remembers your preferences — that's useful on its own. The actions are the reward for building that understanding.
